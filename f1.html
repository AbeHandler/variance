$F_1$ scores are common across natural language processing. Although $F_1$ is easy to compute, the formula is slightly less interpretable than simpler measures like accuracy. This widget tries to build intuition for $F_1$. Recall the formula $F_1= \frac{2}{\frac{1}{P} + \frac{1}{R}} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{P} + \frac{1}{R})} = \frac{1}{M}$. This expression is a harmonic mean: I use the mnemonic ''harmonic mean of precision and recall'' to remember what $F_1$ is.

Drag the red dot to examine different values of precision $P$ and recall $R$, and observe the change in $F_1$. The graph to the right shows the values $\frac{1}{P}$ and $\frac{1}{R}$ as small dots, along with the average of these reciprocals $M$ as a big blue dot. $F_1$ is $\frac{1}{M}$, i.e. 1 divided by the average height of the small blue dots.
